My Design System Journey: Building Crystal

Creating harmony across products through systematic design

[Project Hero Image]

When I joined the team, I noticed our products suffered from inconsistent designs as multiple teams worked in silos. This case study documents my 18-month effort to establish Crystal - a comprehensive design system that brought cohesion to our digital ecosystem while accelerating our workflow.

My Role

  • Lead UX/Visual Designer
  • System Architecture
  • Component Creation
  • Accessibility Advocate
  • Cross-team Collaboration

Project Details

  • Duration: Nov 2022 - Sept 2024
  • Client: Internal Product Teams
  • Tools: Figma, Storybook, Jira
  • Team: 4 Designers, 6 Developers

Discovering the Need for Systemization

[Research Process Visualization]

Through audits of our existing products, I identified 47 variations of buttons, 12 different color systems, and inconsistent spacing patterns. Interviews with 14 designers and developers revealed:

  • 80% of designers spent over 30% of their time recreating existing components
  • Developers reported 15-20% of bugs stemmed from design inconsistencies
  • New team members needed 3-4 weeks to learn all design variations

This research convinced stakeholders to invest in a unified system. I led workshops to define our core principles: Clarity, Consistency, and Scalability.

Structuring the Design Approach

[Design Process Diagram]

I advocated for Atomic Design methodology after evaluating several approaches. Breaking components into atoms, molecules, and organisms created a natural hierarchy that matched our development process.

Foundation First

Before building components, I established:

  • A 4px baseline grid for all measurements
  • An accessible color palette with WCAG AA compliance
  • Type scale with optimal readability ratios
  • Motion guidelines for interactive elements

Component Creation

Each component went through:

  1. Research of industry patterns
  2. Multiple design explorations
  3. Accessibility review
  4. Developer consultation
  5. Documentation in Storybook

Building the Component Library

[Component Library Screenshot]

I created 56 core components with variants, focusing on:

Atoms

  • Buttons with 8 intent states
  • Text inputs with validation
  • Accessible form controls
  • Icon system with 120+ symbols

Molecules & Organisms

  • Data tables with sorting
  • Complex form patterns
  • Navigation systems
  • Feedback components

Measurable Impact

[Metrics Dashboard]

After implementation across three product teams:

  • Design velocity increased by 40%
  • Development time for common UI reduced by 35%
  • Accessibility issues decreased by 60%
  • New hire ramp-up time cut in half

The system continues to evolve, with quarterly reviews to incorporate new patterns and technologies while maintaining consistency.
